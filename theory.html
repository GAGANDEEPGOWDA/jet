THEORY:





  • LDR (LIGHT DEPENDENT RESISTOR).


  • LIGHT DEPENDENT RESISTORS, LDR's ARE ELECTRIC COMPONENTS THAT ARE USED TO DETECT LIGHT AND CHANGE THE OPERATION OF A CIRCUIT DEPENDENT UPON THE LIGHT LEVELS.


  • THEY ARE SPECIFICALLY DESIGNED FOR THEIR LIGHT SENSITIVITY AND THE CHANGE IN RESISTANCE.


  • LDR's ARE MADE FROM SEMICONDUCTOR MATERIALS TO ENABLE THEM TO HAVE THEIR LIGHT SENSITIVE PROPERTIES.


  • THE RESISTANCE OF AN LDR MAY TYPICALLY HAVE THE FOLLOWING RESISTANCE,
    DAYLIGHT = 5000 OHM , DARK = 20000000 OHM.
